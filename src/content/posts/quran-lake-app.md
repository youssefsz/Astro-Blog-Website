Today, I am incredibly proud to announce the release of **QuranLake**, a mobile application dedicated entirely to providing a seamless experience for reading the Quran and tracking daily prayer times.

QuranLake is a comprehensive app that blends modern UX with traditional functionalities to serve users all around the world. Because mobile accessibility is paramount, I built and released this app cross-platform using Flutter—and it is now officially available for both iOS and Android.

## Inspired by the Web

The core inspiration and much of the foundational dataset came from Mansouri Youssef's widely respected web application, [quranlake.com](https://quranlake.com/). I sought to take that stellar web experience and package it into a highly performant, accessible mobile product that feels localized and native in the palm of your hand.

## Features at a Glance

A primary directive while designing QuranLake was clarity and ease of use. Key features of the application include:
- **Comprehensive Quranic Text**: A comfortable layout optimized specifically for mobile displays.
- **Accurate Prayer Times**: Reliable daily prayer calculations natively integrated into the app.
- **Cross-Platform**: Because the goal is accessibility, choosing Flutter allowed me to ship high-performing builds to multiple ecosystems.
- **Beautiful UI**: An immersive but clean interface that prioritizes its core purpose without distraction.

## Fully Open Source

Another important aspect of QuranLake is transparency and community. I have elected to make this application entirely open-source. Anyone who wishes to audit the codebase, fork their own copy, or contribute improvements is encouraged to do so.
