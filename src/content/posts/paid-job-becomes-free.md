In developer groups, it is common to see job posts where the author clearly states that the work is paid. When someone responds by offering to do the same work for free, it creates an unnecessary contradiction.

Even in non-freelance communities, once a paid opportunity is posted and people begin applying, the exchange becomes professional. Offering free work in this context does not help the client, and it does not help the community. It blurs expectations, [devalues the skill involved](https://blog.freelancersunion.org/2024/04/04/the-true-cost-of-free-work/), and shifts attention away from fair collaboration.

Helping others is valuable, but developer communities work best when paid work stays paid, and volunteering is clearly separated from [professional opportunities](/posts/value-of-expertise).
